Ube Macapuno Cake: A Vibrant Filipino Delight

Indulge in the irresistible flavors of the Philippines with Ube Macapuno Cake! This light and fluffy cake boasts a stunning purple hue thanks to ube (purple yam) and is studded with chewy macapuno (coconut sport) for a delightful textural contrast. It's a show-stopping dessert that's sure to impress your guests!

Ingredients

Ube Cake:

  • 2 ½ cups cake flour
  • 3 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup cooked and finely grated ube (purple yam)
  • ¾ cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 7 egg yolks, lightly beaten
  • ½ cup corn syrup
  • ½ cup vegetable oil
  • 7 egg whites
  • 1 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 6 drops red food coloring
  • 6 drops blue food coloring
  • 1 (12 ounce) jar macapuno (coconut preserves)

Buttercream:

  • 1 cup evaporated milk, chilled
  • ¾ cup white sugar
  • 1 cup softened butter
  • 1 drop violet food coloring

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C). Line the bottoms of three 9-inch round pans with parchment paper.
  2. Make the Ube Cake: In a large bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, and salt. Set aside. In another bowl, combine grated ube with ¾ cup milk and vanilla extract. Blend until smooth. Add egg yolks, corn syrup, and oil to the ube mixture. Gradually fold in the dry ingredients until just combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat egg whites with cream of tartar until foamy. Gradually add sugar, then red and blue food coloring, beating until stiff peaks form. Gently fold the egg whites into the ube batter in three batches until no streaks remain. Divide the batter evenly among the prepared pans.
  4. Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Cool cakes completely in pans on a wire rack, then invert and remove. Spread a layer of macapuno preserves between each cake layer.
  5. Make the Buttercream: Combine evaporated milk and sugar in a small saucepan. Heat over low heat until sugar dissolves, then set aside to cool slightly. Cream together softened butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Beat in food coloring until desired shade is reached. Frost the entire cake with buttercream.
